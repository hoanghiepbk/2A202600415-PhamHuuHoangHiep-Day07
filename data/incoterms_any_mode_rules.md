# Incoterms 2020 - Rules for Any Mode(s) of Transport

Source: ICC Incoterms 2020 (compiled from `incorterm.md`)
Language: English
Domain: Incoterms
Section: any_mode

## Included rules

- EXW (Ex Works)
- FCA (Free Carrier)
- CPT (Carriage Paid To)
- CIP (Carriage and Insurance Paid To)
- DAP (Delivered at Place)
- DPU (Delivered at Place Unloaded)
- DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)

## Key notes by rule family

### EXW
- Seller places goods at buyer's disposal.
- Seller has minimum obligations.
- Often risky in international sales if export formalities are unclear.

### FCA
- Seller delivers to carrier at agreed place.
- Better than EXW for many export situations.

### CPT and CIP
- Seller arranges carriage to destination.
- Risk transfers when goods are handed to the carrier, not at final destination.
- Under CIP, seller must also provide insurance with broader cover than CFR/CIF style minimum.

### DAP, DPU, DDP
- Delivery point is near buyer side (destination).
- DPU is the only rule requiring seller to unload at destination.
- DDP gives seller maximum obligations, including import clearance and duties.
