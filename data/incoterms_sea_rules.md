# Incoterms 2020 - Sea and Inland Waterway Rules

Source: ICC Incoterms 2020 (compiled from `incorterm.md`)
Language: English
Domain: Incoterms
Section: sea_inland

## Included rules

- FAS (Free Alongside Ship)
- FOB (Free On Board)
- CFR (Cost and Freight)
- CIF (Cost, Insurance and Freight)

## Rule highlights

### FAS
- Seller delivers when goods are placed alongside vessel at named port of shipment.
- Risk transfers at that point.

### FOB
- Seller delivers when goods are on board vessel at named port of shipment.
- Risk transfers when goods are on board.

### CFR
- Seller pays costs and freight to named destination port.
- Risk transfers earlier (at shipment point), not at destination.

### CIF
- Same shipment-risk logic as CFR.
- Seller must also contract for insurance for buyer's risk during carriage.

## Common confusion points

- "Seller pays freight" does not always mean "seller bears risk until destination."
- In C-rules, cost and risk split at different points.
- Exact named port is critical to avoid disputes.
