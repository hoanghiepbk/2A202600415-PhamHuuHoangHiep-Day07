# Incoterms 2020 - Risk and Cost Focus

Source: ICC Incoterms 2020 (compiled from `incorterm.md`)
Language: English
Domain: Incoterms
Section: risk_cost

## Core principle

Risk transfer point and cost allocation point are central to Incoterms interpretation.
They may align or split differently by rule.

## Key reminders

- A2 defines delivery point/place.
- A3 determines when risk passes from seller to buyer.
- A9/B9 define cost allocation before and after delivery point.
- In many shipment rules, seller may pay some transport costs but risk can transfer earlier.

## High-value distinctions for benchmark

1. **EXW vs DDP**
   - EXW: seller minimum obligations.
   - DDP: seller maximum obligations, including import-side responsibilities.

2. **CPT/CIP vs DAP/DPU**
   - CPT/CIP: seller contracts carriage, risk often transfers at handover to carrier.
   - DAP/DPU: delivery linked to destination side; DPU includes unloading by seller.

3. **CFR/CIF**
   - Seller pays freight to destination.
   - Risk still transfers at shipment stage.
   - CIF adds seller insurance duty.
